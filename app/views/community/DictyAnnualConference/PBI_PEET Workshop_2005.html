PBI and PEET Workshop II
Finding, Identifying, and Culturing Protostelids, Dictyostelids, and Myxomycetes

June 6-13, 2005
Royal Botanic Garden CSIC, Madrid
In conjunction with
The University of Arkansas

During August, 2004, a workshop was hosted at Ernst Moritz Arndt University Greifswald Botanical Institute and Botanical Garden (Germany) by Dr. Martin Schnittler to introduce European biologists to the lesser known eumycetozoans, the protostelids and dictyostelid cellular slime molds. It was supported by National Science Foundation PBI and PEET grants to the University of Arkansas. This first workshop, led by Drs. Fred Spiegel and Jim Cavender, covered methods for collecting, plating, and identifying these mycetozoans from primary isolation plates. During the workshop, several participants expressed a desire to have a follow-up workshop to cover more advanced areas such as the isolation and culturing of protostelids and dictyostelids.

Dr. Carlos Lado will host this second workshop at the Royal Botanic Garden CSIC, Madrid, from June 6-13, 2005. The topics will include a quick review of techniques for collecting and plating substrates for dictyostelids and protostelids and for identifying these slime molds. The workshop will then move on to cover aspects of trophic cell morphology in protostelids and methods for establishing single slime mold cultures of dictyostelids, protostelids, and, as a new topic, myxomycetes. The ability to culture reliably identified strains of eumycetozoans is vitally important to those working on the systematics of the group. If possible, there will be a demonstration of how to photograph these mycetozoans using Auto-Montage.

The coverage of protostelids will be led by Dr. Fred Spiegel and John Shadwick of the University of Arkansas, dictyostelids by Dr. Jim Cavender of Ohio University, and myxomycetes by Dr. Ed Haskins of the University of Washington, emeritus.

As with the first workshop, the maximum number of participants is 20. The workshop is principally but not exclusively for European participants. There will be no registration fee for the workshop. We are working to find reasonably priced housing for participants near the Garden.

If you are interested in further information on the workshop, please contact Fred Spiegel (fspiegel@uark.edu), John Shadwick (jshadw@uark.edu), or Carlos Lado (lado@ma-rjb.csic.es).
